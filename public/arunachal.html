Arunachal Pradesh, located in the northeastern part of India, is often referred to as the "Land of the Rising Sun" because it is the first Indian state to witness the sunrise. It shares international borders with China (Tibet) to the north, Myanmar to the east, and Bhutan to the west, making it a strategically important state for India. It also shares its boundaries with the Indian states of Assam and Nagaland. This beautiful state is known for its stunning landscapes, diverse tribal cultures, dense forests, and serene monasteries. The region remains relatively unexplored, preserving its natural beauty and indigenous traditions.

History of Arunachal Pradesh

Arunachal Pradesh has a long history dating back to ancient times. According to Hindu mythology, it is believed that Sage Parashurama washed away his sins in the Lohit River, and Sage Vyasa meditated in the hills of Arunachal. The region also finds mention in the Mahabharata and Ramayana, with King Bhishmaka of the Idu Mishmi tribe being associated with the legend of Krishna and Rukmini. Historically, the area was under the rule of different local chieftains and tribes before the British took control in the 19th century. Geography and Natural Beauty

Arunachal Pradesh is covered with thick forests, snow-capped mountains, and deep river valleys. The Himalayan ranges run across the state, with Kangto Peak (7,090 m) being the highest mountain. The Siang, Subansiri, Kameng, and Lohit rivers flow through the region, providing lifelines to the local communities. The climate varies from subtropical in the foothills to alpine in the mountains. Winters can be quite cold, especially in high-altitude areas, while summers remain mild. The state also experiences heavy rainfall during the monsoon season.
