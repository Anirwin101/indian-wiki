History of Surat

Surat has a rich history dating back to the Mughal era. It was once a major port city during the 16th and 17th centuries, attracting traders from Europe and the Middle East. The city flourished under Mughal rule and later became a key center for the British East India Company. Today, Surat continues to thrive as a commercial powerhouse.

Geography and Modern Development

Located on the banks of the Tapi River, Surat enjoys a coastal advantage that has historically contributed to its prosperity. The city has witnessed rapid industrialization, with IT hubs, smart city initiatives, and sustainable urban planning making it a model for modern development.

Cultural and Culinary Heritage

Surat is famous for its unique blend of Gujarati culture and global influences. The city is renowned for its street food, including Locho, Surati Undhiyu, and Ghari sweets. Festivals like Navratri and Diwali are celebrated with grandeur, making Surat a lively and colorful city.
