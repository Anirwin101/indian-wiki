Kolkata, formerly known as Calcutta, is the capital city of West Bengal. It is a major cultural, economic, and educational hub in Eastern India. Known for its colonial architecture, vibrant festivals, and iconic Howrah Bridge, Kolkata is a city that beautifully blends tradition and modernity.

History of Kolkata

Kolkata has a rich history that dates back to the British colonial era. It was the capital of British India until 1911. The city is home to historical landmarks like Victoria Memorial, Indian Museum, and St. Paul's Cathedral.

Geography and Modern Development

Located on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River, Kolkata is known for its tropical climate and bustling urban life. The city has seen rapid development in infrastructure, including IT hubs, metro rail, and modern highways, making it a key economic hub in India.

Cultural and Culinary Heritage

Kolkata is a cultural treasure trove, famous for its literature, art, and vibrant festivals like Durga Puja. The city is also a food lover's paradise, offering delicacies like rasgulla, mishti doi, kathi rolls, and fish curry.
