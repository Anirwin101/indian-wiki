Chennai, formerly known as Madras, is the capital city of Tamil Nadu. It is a major cultural, economic, and educational hub in South India. Known for its rich heritage, classical music, and iconic Marina Beach, Chennai is a city that beautifully blends tradition and modernity.

History of Chennai

Chennai has a rich history that dates back to the 1st century. It was an important center for trade and commerce during the colonial era. The city is home to historical landmarks like Fort St. George, Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and San Thome Basilica.

Geography and Modern Development

Located on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal, Chennai is known for its tropical climate and bustling urban life. The city has seen rapid development in infrastructure, including IT parks, metro rail, and modern highways, making it a key economic hub in India.

Cultural and Culinary Heritage

Chennai is a cultural treasure trove, famous for its classical Carnatic music, Bharatanatyam dance, and vibrant festivals like Pongal and Navaratri. The city is also a food lover's paradise, offering delicacies like idli, dosa, filter coffee, and Chettinad cuisine.
