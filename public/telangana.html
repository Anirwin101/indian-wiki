Telangana, located in southern India, is the youngest state of the country, formed in 2014. It is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and rapid development. Famous for its iconic Charminar, delicious Hyderabadi biryani, and vibrant festivals, Telangana is a blend of tradition and modernity.

History of Telangana

Telangana has a rich historical legacy, with roots tracing back to the Satavahana dynasty. It was an important region during the reign of the Kakatiyas, Qutb Shahis, and the Nizams of Hyderabad. The state played a significant role in India's history and is home to several historical monuments and landmarks.

Geography and Natural Beauty

Telangana is characterized by its diverse geography, including the Deccan Plateau, lush forests, and scenic waterfalls. The Godavari and Krishna rivers flow through the state, enriching its agricultural lands. Its natural beauty and historical sites attract tourists from across the globe. It also has the World's Largest Film City, The Ramoji Film City

Cultural and Linguistic Identity

Telangana is known for its vibrant culture, traditional crafts, and unique cuisine. The state celebrates festivals like Bonalu, Bathukamma, and Sankranti with great enthusiasm. The primary languages spoken in Telangana include Telugu (official) and Urdu.
