History of Kochi

Kochi has a fascinating history that dates back to ancient times when it was a major center for the spice trade. The city gained prominence in the 14th century when it became a key port for Arab, Chinese, and European traders. The Portuguese arrived in Kochi in 1503, making it the first European colony in India. They built the famous St. Francis Church, which still stands as a testament to their influence. Later, the Dutch and the British took control of the city, leaving behind architectural and cultural imprints. Fort Kochi, with its colonial-era buildings, is a living museum of this rich history. Post-independence, Kochi emerged as a modern city while preserving its historical charm. Today, it is a thriving metropolis known for its maritime trade, IT parks, and cultural festivals like the Kochi-Muziris Biennale, which attracts artists and art lovers from across the globe.
