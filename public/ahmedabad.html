History of Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad was founded in 1411 by Sultan Ahmed Shah. The city is home to iconic landmarks like the Sabarmati Ashram, Jama Masjid, and Sidi Saiyyed Mosque, which reflect its historical and architectural significance.

Geography and Modern Development

As Ahmedabad is the largest city of Gujarat, it has witnessed rapid development in infrastructure, technology, tourism, and education. As part of the Smart Cities Mission, projects like smart traffic management, waste disposal systems, and digital public transport have been implemented. The Ahmedabad Metro Phase 2 expansion is planned to improve connectivity. Roads, flyovers, and underpasses have been upgraded to ease traffic congestion. IT and startup ecosystems are growing, with the emergence of tech hubs and co-working spaces. Tourism remains a major economic driver, with heritage conservation projects and improved facilities enhancing visitor experiences. The city is also becoming an education hub with institutions like IIM Ahmedabad, NID, and Gujarat University attracting students. Healthcare infrastructure has expanded with new hospitals and better medical facilities. E-commerce, retail, and real estate sectors are also growing, making Ahmedabad a well-rounded, developing urban center.

Cultural and Culinary Heritage

Ahmedabad is a cultural hub, famous for its traditional Gujarati music, dance, and festivals like Navratri and Uttarayan. The city is also renowned for its cuisine, offering dishes like dhokla, fafda, and khaman.
